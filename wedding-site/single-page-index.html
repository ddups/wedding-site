Friday

  1. 4:00 pm
  2. 7:00 pm
  3. 9:00 pm
  1. Open Check-In

    Stop by the main office any time after 4 pm and grab your info bag. This will contain a map of the camp, as well as some other information that will also be listed on the bulletin board. Find your cabin and get yourself settled, and then the camp is yours to explore!

  2. L'Après Dîner

    Join us in the Rec Center after the rehearsal dinner for drinks, dancing, and mingling! This will be super casual so feel free to make your own plans for dinner and show up whenever.

  3. S'mores and Ghost Stories

    Head over to the boy's quad and grab a spot by the fire pit. Kick back and relax, enjoy some toasty treats, and swap stories with old friends and new ones.

  1. Brunch

    Brunch at the rec hall. Have some breakfast and experiment at the mimosa bar!

  2. Pickup Ultimate

    Pick-up Ultimate game in the boy's quad. Everyone is encouraged to come play or watch.

  3. Open Camp

    Open camp!! Go canoeing, play some disc golf or any of the other games and activities.

  4. Dress to Impress

    Time to get ready and head to the Rec Hall lawn for some pre-freshments, then hit the beach.

  5. WE GET MARRIED!!

    Make sure to bring your sunglasses, cameras, and some tissues!

  6. Cocktail Hour

    Meet up on the Rec Hall lawn for appetizers, drinks, and a chance to chat with the newly-weds.

  7. Dinner is Served

    We will start with wedding party introductions, dance for the first time as a married couple, and listen to some people sing our praises. Then we will get to the real reason you came... the dinner buffet!

  8. Dance Dance Revolution

    Time to put on your dancing shoes, grab a partner, and groove the night away.

  9. Post-Nuptual Party Time!

    Come to a bon fire in the woods or crank up your own music and feel free to keep the party rocking all night!

  1. Hangover Helper

    Cure your hangover with bacon and eggs! And a little hair of the dog, of course...

  2. Clean Up, Clean Up...

    We hate to ask it, but there will be a lot of work to do to get the camp cleaned up. If you could spare at least a little of your time and help us, it would be the best wedding gift of all (other than large sums of cash)

  3. That's a Wrap Folks!

    Time to pack up and ship out... the party is over. We hope that you will be leaving with sore legs, some great memories, and at least some your dignity still intact!

The Venue

Camp Cody is located on the shores of Ossipee Lake — two hours north of Boston. If you use your GPS, you may end up being directed to the second driveway, so when you get onto Ossipee Lake Rd keep your eyes open for the balloons and signs and take a right down the first driveway.

Please plan on parking in the main lot when you arrive. If you are going to be staying the night, you can move to one of the other lots closer to your cabin. If you are going to be leaving at some point on Saturday you can remain parked in the main lot.

The title "Best Man" could not be more applicable to anyone that I know. For 27 years I have had the pleasure -- and sometimes pain -- of having CJ as my brother. We have always had a strong bond, even though growing up we were often on each other's nerves. I have witnessed CJ grow into an amazing person and our friendship has only gotten stronger through the years. From playing with our Lego "Buddies" to staying up late on Christmas playing our new video game, we always had each other when there was no one else around. We have weathered many bad times and enjoyed many good ones, and I could not ask for a better person to be by my side on my wedding day.

Tyler is everything a man could ask for in a friend: trustworthy, dependable, honest... the list goes on. My first memory of Tyler is from high school when he replaced Mr. Hill's eraser/rag with a rubber chicken, which he then used to erase the board. Though we were not introduced until later, this scene sticks out in my mind as an encapsulation of Tyler's personality. I have no reservations around him and have always felt comfortable being my true, weird self. We were inseparable throughout high school and now refuse to let time pull us apart. More like a brother to me than a friend, I consider myself very lucky to have Tyler as one of my groomsmen.

One of my oldest and closest friends, Brian and I have been best buds from the start. We met in French class in our freshman year of high school and became immediate friends the following year. We bonded over our love for Celine Dion and creating elaborate fictional tales. Brian introduced me to ultimate which helped me come out of my shell. Along with Tyler, Brian and I spent many summers just being silly, carefree kids. He is one of the most hard working people I have ever met, yet he is still the fun-loving kid I have always known. No matter how long we go without seeing each other it feels like it was just yesterday. I'm honored to call Brian a groomsman and one of my best friends.

I first met my future brother-in-law and fellow goof-ball, Jan, at UNH. He was dating Emma's sister, now his wife, Rachel. From the start I knew Jan and I would get along. He is smart, determined, and sometimes very serious -- and those aren't even all the things we have in common! Jan has an adventure streak a mile wide and knows how to get the most out of life. He always does his research and his charming nature gets him the best deals on everything from gear, to flights, to limousines in Las Vegas. Though Jan is usually generous and caring, when there is a board game involved all bets are off! I'm really glad to have Jan as a friend, groomsman, and soon-to-be brother.

Out of all the friends I made in college, Jake sticks out as one of the best. Though he is actually younger than me, I have often looked up to him -- in a figurative sense. We share many passions: software development, ultimate frisbee, fantasy football, burritos... It is no wonder at all how we became such close friends. His sense of humor and laugh are infectious and could bring someone out of the worst of moods. Living with him in college allowed me to see just how great of a person Jake is. He is always willing to listen and lend a hand when needed. All of these things made Jake an easy choice for me, and I am happy to have him as part of my wedding.

I have known Eric since elementary school, though we didn't become good friends until the middle of high school when we played ultimate together. Together we have been described by others as "loud" or "boisterous" or "the worst two people to have a discussion with in an enclosed space". In the latter half of high school I spent many summer days doing flips into Eric's pool or on his trampoline. From sports, to cards, to getting in trouble, our shared interests made our friendship grow like a weed. Many people aren't lucky enough to have one close friend from high school, but along with Brian and Tyler I feel privileged to have three as my groomsmen.

Easily one of the funniest people I have ever known, Brady and I have been close ever since he burst onto the UNH Ultimate scene. We bonded over long car rides to creepy motels, jumping on beds, and cooking dinner for an entire Ultimate team. Brady introduced me to the finer things in life like Tim and Eric, belly drumming, the RIGHT way to play Guesstures, and of course Beezer. Having him as my captain for years, I have seen the best that Brady has to offer. He is organized, dependable, but most of all he knows how to have a good time. I strive to be as care-free as he is able to be. When it came time to pick out my groomsmen, Brady was an easy choice.
